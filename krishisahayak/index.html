Our Mission

Crop residue burning during the winter months in Haryana and Punjab has been a persistent issue leading to severe pollution, posing health hazards for millions of people in Delhi and neighboring states.The severe air pollution caused by crop residue burning in Haryana and Punjab has far-reaching effects beyond these states' borders. The toxic air masses travel to neighboring states like Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, and Rajasthan, significantly worsening air quality in these regions. Consequently, millions of people living in Delhi and nearby states suffer from deteriorating air quality during the winter months, leading to a public health crisis.
